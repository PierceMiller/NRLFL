So I have been thinking a little bit more about the Sharks, and I think Jack Williams ($472k) might be a viable/good option this year. Assuming that Magoulias spends about 20 minutes at hooker with the injury to King, it is entirely possible, if not the most likely scenario, that the Sharks carry an outside back on the bench given they have a number of injury prone players and a glut of outside backs.

I am expecting the Sharks team to look like: Moylan, Dugan, Xerri, Ramien, Morris, Townsend, SJ, Fifi (50), Brailey (60), Woods (45), Graham (80), Nikora (80), Williams (50), Magoulias (50 - 30L/20H), Uele (40), Rudolf/Bukuya (25), Ronaldo (injury cover),

The reality is that the Sharks have the players with the minutes in them to be able to run a 3 forward bench pretty comfortably and given the history of Moylan, Dugan and SJ they would be mad not to carry an outside back now with Capewell gone. Obviously they could choose to move Nikora and carry Sorenson on the bench but I would be surprised if Ronaldo doesn't demand selection. We might even see a situation where a Dugan or Morris doesnt play 80 routinely to allow for Ronaldo's selection.

Word out of the sharks is that Williams has been training the proverbial house down and killing it physically in the gym. Wade Graham is quoted last week as saying “I’m really keen to see Jack Williams this year. He’s been on the cusp of really being something special in the NRL but of course he just sat behind ‘Gal’ for a few years.".

Williams could very well be in for a huge breakout season and represents a pretty low risk buy with plenty of upside if a guy like Fifita went down long term. Worst case he averages in the low 40s, best case with a 55-60 minute role he could be a borderline keeper.
